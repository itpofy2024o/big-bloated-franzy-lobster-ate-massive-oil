# News Analysis: US Redirecting Iranian Assets to Gulf Allies

**Headline:** US wants to send frozen Iranian assets to Gulf allies – media
**Summary:** The Trump administration is exploring the use of frozen Iranian assets to fund the rebuilding of energy and infrastructure in Gulf Arab states that have sustained damage from Iranian-backed retaliatory strikes. US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent is reportedly directing officials to assess the damage inflicted on US allies in the region. This move comes amid stalled truce negotiations, where Tehran is demanding access to approximately $24 billion in frozen funds as a precondition for a lasting peace deal. The initiative aims to ensure that any future destruction caused by Iran can be remediated through its own assets. This policy shift highlights the administration's approach to using financial leverage to manage geopolitical tensions and support regional stability.
**Trade Relevance:** Significant impact on energy markets and regional stability in the Persian Gulf. Potential volatility in oil and gas futures depending on the progress of truce talks and the scale of infrastructure damage. The reallocation of assets could also impact sovereign debt and regional investment climates.
